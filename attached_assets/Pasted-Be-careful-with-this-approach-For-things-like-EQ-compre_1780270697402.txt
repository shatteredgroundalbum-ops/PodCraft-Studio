Be careful with this approach.

For things like EQ, compression, de-essing, noise reduction, and mastering, there is no single golden setting that works for every voice. A deep male voice, a female voice, a USB microphone, an SM7B, a noisy room, and a treated room all need different settings.

What you can do is define:

Minimum (too little)

Target Range (gold zone)

Maximum (too much / danger zone)


That is exactly how professional engineers usually think.

These are the kinds of ranges I'd have the AI Producer use. Instead of hard-coding exact values, the AI should aim for the gold zone and adjust based on the actual voice, microphone, room, and recording conditions. That will produce far more professional results than forcing the same settings on every recording.
